Marketing & AI in Asia-Pacific: how marketing teams use Claude

An interactive dashboard analysing how marketers across 18 Asia-Pacific markets use AI, based on the Anthropic Economic Index. It covers three questions: how intensely each market has adopted AI, which marketing roles use it to automate versus collaborate, and what kind of content gets produced. Enable JavaScript to use the interactive charts.

Content creation leads almost everywhere in APAC

Content creation and copywriting is the largest single use of Claude in 15 of the 18 published markets, and a top-two use in all 18, ranging from about 20% of usage in Cambodia to 32% in Indonesia. The exceptions: software development leads in Cambodia, and education leads in Nepal and Mongolia. Globally the figure is 22.7%, and it grew in 14 of 18 APAC markets between April and May 2026.

The automation gradient by marketing role

Marketing roles split cleanly between automation (delegating whole tasks) and augmentation (iterating with AI):

  • Automation-leaning: Search Marketing Strategists (~55% of tasks delegated) and Market Research Analysts (~46%).
  • Balanced: Marketing Managers and PR Specialists (~38-39%).
  • Augmentation-leaning: Advertising & Promotions Managers (~33%), Graphic Designers (~30%), and Writers & Authors (~29%), who work mostly through back-and-forth iteration.

Adoption clusters

Established markets, Australia (6.4× adoption index), Singapore (5.8×), New Zealand (4.8×), South Korea (3.8×), show high, work-heavy usage. Emerging markets such as the Philippines and Pakistan have lower overall adoption but the region's highest marketing-content output, with the Philippines producing marketing and social artifacts at 4.4% of usage versus a 2.6% global benchmark.

About the data

Source: the Anthropic Economic Index, April–May 2026 release, covering Claude.ai chat and Cowork usage (Free, Pro, and Max). It excludes enterprise API usage and other AI tools. Occupation and topic labels are inferred from conversation content, not verified job titles; time-saved figures are model estimates. Released under CC-BY.
